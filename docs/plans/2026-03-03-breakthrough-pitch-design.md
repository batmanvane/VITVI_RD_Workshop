# Design: Breakthrough Pitch — Second PhD Presentation Track

## Summary

Extend the workshop's PhD presentation format from a single "Problem Pitch" (roadblock-focused) to a dual-track system: students choose either a **Problem Pitch** or a **Breakthrough Pitch** depending on where they are in their research. This maps directly to the workshop subtitle — "Roadblocks, Methods, and Breakthroughs."

- **Problem Pitch** = the unsolved side (existing format, unchanged)
- **Breakthrough Pitch** = the solved side (new format)

Both live under the "Anti-Pitch" umbrella: honest, unpolished, technically grounded.

## Breakthrough Pitch Format

### Time Box

Same as Problem Pitch: **30 minutes total** (10 min presentation + 20 min group discussion).

### 5-Slide Rule

| Slide | Time | Content |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1. Context | 2 min | Overarching research goal |
| 2. The Finding | 2 min | The core insight or result you want to share |
| 3. How You Got There | 3 min | The method, the path, what didn't work first |
| 4. Why It Matters | 1.5 min | Significance, implications, limitations you already see |
| 5. The Challenge | 1.5 min | Specific prompts for the room: "Where does this break? Where else could this apply?" |

### Discussion Structure (20 min)

Two phases:

1. **Stress-Test (~10 min):** The room plays devil's advocate. Challenge the methodology, question assumptions, poke holes. Goal: help the presenter understand the limits of their finding.
2. **Transfer (~10 min):** Flip the energy. Where else could this apply? Other domains, other students' problems, industry use cases. Goal: cross-pollination across the group.

## Problem Pitch Format (Existing, Unchanged)

### 4-Slide Rule

| Slide | Time | Content |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1. Context | 2 min | Overarching goal |
| 2. Core Roadblock | 3 min | 1-2 specific technical problems |
| 3. What I've Tried | 3 min | Failed solutions and why |
| 4. The Ask | 2 min | Specific questions for the room's brain-trust |

### Discussion: 20 min open troubleshooting (unchanged)

## Track Choice

Students indicate their track preference **at registration**. This allows scheduling mixed blocks (Problem and Breakthrough pitches in the same session).
