# Project Context: VITVI R&D Workshop 2026

## Core Objective
This project manages the planning and assets for the **VITVI R&D Workshop 2026: Roadblocks, Methods, and Breakthroughs**. 
The event is a 2-day intensive technical workshop bringing together mid-thesis PhD students, industry R&D professionals, and startup founders. 

## Vibe & Philosophy
* **Format:** Active, roll-up-your-sleeves, "messy middle" troubleshooting. 
* **Tone:** Radical candor. A safe space to share broken code, failed experiments, and dirty data.
* **Anti-Goal:** This is NOT a traditional academic symposium. High-level, polished thesis presentations are actively discouraged.

## Event Mechanics
* **Attendees:** ~12 PhD students + Industry/Startup experts.
* **Location:** Offsite hotel (1-2 hours drive for attendees).
* **Impulse Talks:** 4 total (Academia, Startup, Industry, Deep-Dive/Methodology). Spread throughout the 2 days to maintain energy levels. Industry talk is specifically scheduled for Day 2 morning.

## The "Anti-Pitch" PhD Format
Each student chooses one of two tracks depending on where they are in their research. Both share the same 30-minute time box. Track choice is made at registration.

### Problem Pitch (Roadblock Track)
* **Total Time:** 30 minutes per student.
* **Breakdown:** 10-minute presentation + 20-minute group troubleshooting.
* **Slide Constraints (Strict 4-Slide Rule):**
  1. Context (2 mins): Overarching goal.
  2. Core Roadblock (3 mins): 1-2 specific technical problems.
  3. What I've Tried (3 mins): Failed solutions and why.
  4. The Ask (2 mins): Specific questions for the room's brain-trust.

### Breakthrough Pitch (Finding Track)
* **Total Time:** 30 minutes per student.
* **Breakdown:** 10-minute presentation + 20-minute group discussion.
* **Slide Constraints (5-Slide Rule):**
  1. Context (2 mins): Overarching goal.
  2. The Finding (2 mins): The core insight or result you want to share.
  3. How You Got There (3 mins): The method, the path, what didn't work first.
  4. Why It Matters (1.5 mins): Significance, implications, limitations you see.
  5. The Challenge (1.5 mins): Specific prompts for the room — "Where does this break? Where else could this apply?"
* **Discussion Structure:** ~10 min stress-test (challenge methodology, poke holes) + ~10 min transfer (where else could this apply?).
